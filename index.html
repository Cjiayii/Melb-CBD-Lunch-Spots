Five Japanese Lunch Spots in Melbourne CBD That Office Workers Will Actually Return To

Some lunches are just functional. These are not.

If your workday lives somewhere between a calendar full of meetings and a half-hour break that disappears too quickly, the best lunch spots do three things well: they’re reliable, they’re fast enough for the CBD rhythm, and they leave you feeling like you ate something worth stepping out for.

The five places below are my current editorial top picks for Japanese lunch in Melbourne CBD, ranked for a mix of city-worker practicality, strong public review coverage, and value cues in current public sources.

The result is a list that leans warm, comforting, and quietly addictive: glossy udon, deeply savoury ramen, sushi that moves fast, and one very good omurice for the days you want something a little different.

The Five

1) Yamamoto Udon

A$19–25 Melbourne CBD Best for solo lunch

There’s something especially appealing about a lunch spot that feels calm without being slow. Yamamoto Udon stands out as a highly focused CBD udon specialist, with food arriving quickly and a space that feels well-suited to solo diners.

What to order first

Wagyu mapo tofu udon or beef udon with tempura.

Why it works

A polished, efficient lunch that resets your day without slowing it down.

2) Hakata Gensuke

Up to A$20 Russell Street Classic ramen

Some lunches are about comfort; this one is about conviction. Hakata Gensuke remains one of Melbourne’s best-known ramen spots, especially for its rich tonkotsu broth and customisable bowls.

What to order first

Black tonkotsu or God Fire ramen, plus gyoza.

3) Mr Ramen San

Under A$20 MidCity Centre

If you want something dependable and genuinely repeatable, this is it. The broth is lighter, the noodles are house-made, and the whole experience feels easy to return to.

What to order first

Tonkotsu ramen or mazesoba.

4) Tokyo Motto

A$12–20 Bourke Street

For the days when ramen feels too predictable, Tokyo Motto offers something more playful — especially its tornado omurice and Japanese curry dishes.

What to order first

Tornado omurice or Golden curry.

5) Sushi Hotaru

A$13–20 Mid City Centre

Not every lunch needs to be heavy. Sushi Hotaru is fast, flexible, and easy — perfect when your break is short but you still want variety.

What to order first

Soft-shell crab sushi, wagyu, or grilled eel.
